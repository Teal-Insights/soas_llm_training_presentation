Mental Model: The “Jagged Frontier”

What is AI good at?

From Dell’Acqua et al 2023

Inside the frontier: AI excels
Outside the frontier: AI struggles
The challenge: The boundary is invisible, unpredictable, and moving quickly

The Jagged Frontier in Action

Single prompt: “Make a painting of a dumpster fire in the style of Monet”

One hour of prompting: “Make a better jagged frontier visualization”

This is the jagged frontier: Seemingly similar tasks with wildly different AI performance

Understanding the Frontier

Inside (AI excels)

  • Summarizing complex texts quickly
  • Pattern recognition across documents
  • First-draft generation
  • Code generation

Outside (AI struggles)

  • Understanding implicit context
  • Making ethical judgments
  • Recognizing what’s missing
  • Cultural and political subtext

Only YOU can find the “jagged frontier” for tasks that matter to you

The Power and the Problem

Large Language Models are powerful but not clairvoyant

  • Powerful: Can handle complex tasks that take humans hours or days
  • Not clairvoyant: Can’t read your mind or guess what you really need
  • Need sufficient information: The more context you provide, the better the output
  • Key insight: Quality of output depends heavily on quality of input

The Difference Context Makes

Without context: Generic, surface-level responses
With context: Targeted, sophisticated assistance

Two Powerful Prompt Enhancers

Add these to any prompt for better results:

  1. “Please ask me clarifying questions” - Encourages AI to seek additional information rather than make assumptions
  2. “Tell me when you’re unsure about something” - Reduces hallucinations by encouraging admission of uncertainty

These simple additions can save you from misleading responses

Common Prompting Mistakes

❌ What Doesn’t Work

  • Treating AI like Google
  • Vague requests: “Help me with X”
  • No quality control
  • Overwhelming with too much at once

✅ What Works

  • Provide documents + ask for analysis
  • Specific tasks + output format
  • Ask for sources and reasoning
  • Break complex tasks into steps

The Art of Iterative Prompting

AI works best through conversation, not one-shot requests

  • Start broad: Get initial direction and framework
  • Drill down: Ask for specifics on the most interesting parts
  • Refine: Request adjustments to tone, format, or focus
  • Verify: Ask for sources, evidence, or reasoning

Example flow: Research question → Initial analysis → Specific methodology → Alternative approaches → Validation strategy

Prompt Chaining for Complex Tasks

Break complex requests into sequential steps:

❌ All at once

“Analyze this email thread, identify key issues, and draft a response”

✅ Step by step

  1. First: Analyze and clarify
  2. Then: Draft based on analysis

Why this works: Each step can be optimized, you maintain quality control, AI has clearer focus

The “Vibe Coding” Problem

What is “vibe coding”?

AI generates code that looks professional and runs without errors, but:

  • Produces incorrect results
  • Uses functions that don’t exist
  • Mixes syntax from different languages
  • Works for simple cases but fails with real data

The 0-to-90% problem: AI quickly gets you 90% there, then you spend hours debugging the remaining 10%

Best Practices for Research Coding

Strategic approach:

  1. Use most advanced models - Pay for premium access ($20/month is worth it)
  2. Start with your own expertise - Test AI on code you know well
  3. Provide complete context - Include data structure, expected outputs, error messages
  4. Break complex tasks into steps - Don’t ask for entire analysis at once
  5. Always validate outputs - Check results against known answers

Case Study: Using LLMs at Scale

What we’re exploring: How AI enables policy-relevant research at scale

The Policy Challenge

Understanding China’s Role in Financing the Energy Transition

  • China: Key source of lending to developing countries
  • Policy pronouncements about “Green Belt and Road Initiative”
  • Emphasis on “small and beautiful” projects
  • Shift from policy banks to commercial co-financing

Critical questions: Was China actually supporting green transition? Who was participating? What scale?

The Classification Challenge

18,000 Chinese overseas lending projects from AidData’s database

Categories needed:

  • 🟢 Green: Solar, wind, hydro, nuclear
  • 🟫 Brown: Coal, oil, fossil fuels
  • 🔘 Grey: Indirect impacts (transmission)
  • ⚪ Neutral: Non-energy projects

Traditional approach:

  • 1,500 hours of work
  • $22,500 in research costs
  • Large grant funding required

Our result: Completed in 15 hours for $1.58

The Reality of Human vs. LLM Classification

Let’s be honest about manual classification at scale:

  • After a few hours, your eyes glaze over
  • Are you applying criteria consistently?
  • Are you coding the same way as yesterday? Last week?
  • Research assistants face the same challenges

LLMs bring endless patience and consistency

  • Apply same criteria to project 17,000 as to project 1
  • No fatigue, no drift in standards, no bad days
  • Your LLM didn’t stay out partying until 4 AM

Key insight: Not about perfection, but about good-enough accuracy with perfect consistency

Why Keywords Failed, Context Succeeded

Example project: “Development of 500MW solar power plant with backup diesel generator”

❌ Keyword Search

  • Sees “diesel”
  • Classifies as brown
  • Misses primary purpose

✅ LLM Context Understanding

  • Reads entire description
  • Understands primary purpose
  • Recognizes backup as auxiliary
  • Correctly classifies as green

The advantage: LLMs understand context that keyword searches miss entirely

What Made Our Approach Work

Multi-shot prompting: Showed AI how to reason through complex cases

Structured output: Required JSON format with confidence levels

Confidence calibration: AI assessed its own certainty

  • HIGH: Clear alignment with categories
  • MEDIUM: Some ambiguity but reasonable certainty
  • LOW: Significant uncertainty - flag for human review

Key insight: When AI said it was uncertain, it usually was

Validation Strategy

Step 1: Inter-Model Agreement

  • High agreement (85-95%) on most categories
  • Deepseek v3 emerged as best performer

Step 2: Human Validation Benchmark

  • 300 projects manually classified by experts
  • 91.8% agreement with human ratings
  • 95.5% accuracy on green projects specifically
